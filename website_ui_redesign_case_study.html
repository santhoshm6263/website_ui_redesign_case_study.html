Case study — problem → solution → result

Problem

The Thiranex student portal suffered from poor visual hierarchy, lack of urgency signaling, inaccessible modals, and no progressive disclosure — leading to cognitive overload for beginner users unsure of where to focus next.

Research

Evaluated the UI against Nielsen's 10 heuristics. Identified 4 critical issues (system status, user control, aesthetics, accessibility) and 3 warnings (consistency, recognition, flexibility).

Design decisions

Chose a navy + blue + teal palette for trust and clarity. Playfair Display for authority; DM Sans for readability. Introduced urgency color coding (coral/amber/teal badges), a hero progress banner, and accordion task cards to reduce visual clutter.

Result

A cleaner, accessible, responsive student portal with clear information hierarchy, visual urgency, and delightful micro-interactions — ready for Figma handoff or HTML/CSS implementation.
